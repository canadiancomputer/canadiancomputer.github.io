Rental Process


Skip to Return Process

Setting up a rental requires two documents. A Rental Allocation Order and a Rental Fee Order. The Rental Allocation Order is used to reserve the product being rented, and communicate the value of the items being rented to the customer. The Rental Fee Order is invoiced to charge the customer for the rental. Below are instructions for setting up both documents.

To determine the Rental Rate, please use the calculator here

Rentals during repairs will be offered to customers for $99 for the duration of the repair. The rest of the process remains unchanged.

To set up a Rental Allocation Order, do the following:

  • Create a new Order, and assign "Carbon/Core 1 Rental Order" as the customer on this order.
  • Add any physical products being rented to the customer, ensuring each is accurately listed.
  • Add a hyphen and the customer's name after the Carbon/Core 1 Rental Order name. (e.g. Carbon Computing Rental Order - Nathan Arfin)
  • Put the duration into the "PO" field
  • Reserve inventory on this order
  • Add any special instructions for internal use into the internal notes of this order.

Example of a properly completed Allocation Order


To Set up a Rental Fee Order, do the following:

  • Create a new order, and assign the client renting the equipment as the customer on this order.
  • Add the RENT product code and enter a sell price that equals the rental amount.
  • Enter the description and serial number of what is being rented on the next line. (eg: iMac - MC508RN, SN: C02xxxxxxx)
  • Enter the duration of the rental to the next line. (eg: 1 Week, Due back Nov. 23, 2011) â€“ Customers must provide 24hrs notice should they require an extension.
  • Enter the Rental Allocation Order# to the next line. (eg: O-21234)
  • Add any shipping or additional items the customer wants as necessary.
  • Take a deposit in the amount of the Rental Fee on the order.

Enter the Due Date in Rental Calendar

Take a Pre-Authorization, Capture Customer ID, and Complete Rental Agreement

Final Steps

  • Link the two orders together - Gearbox - Link This Document
  • Take all completed paperwork to the Inventory manager for approval and review

A note about accessories

Inventory will remove all manuals and software from the product, as well as common small accessories, or additional packaging by default. These are removed in order to make sure that they are available to re-pack with the product when it is returned. Often these items get lost, and are not required for the rental unit to operate. If you require one of these accessories for the rental, you should be sure to include special instructions in the internal notes of your allocation order.

A note about Rental selection

For Rental units, always pick a Rental Unit (RN Suffix), Used, or open box product (OP Suffix). Do not use any new, factory sealed product for rentals. If the unit being rented is to replace a client's machine which is in service for repair, please ensure that the unit being rented is of equal or lower specifications (and value) than what the customer has left in service for repair. Best efforts will be made to come as close as possible to the unit being checked in.

Rental Return

Retail Inspection

  • Ensure all expected items are present
  • Inspect for new scrapes, scratches, dents, or other damage
  • Assist the customer in signing out of Find My
  • Erase customer data from the device

Complete Documents

  • Invoice the Rental Fee Order
  • If the customer returns the unit on time, this will be fully paid.
  • If the customer has not returned on time, add SKU "RENT-LATE"
  • Calculate the Late Rental Fee in the Rental Calculator
  • Create SRO for Rental Return, link to the Allocation Order

Technicians

  • Perform internal inspection
  • Run ASD MRI, EFI, and OS
  • Verify customer information erased
  • Inform management of any previously un-noted deficiencies
  • Release inventory and mark Allocation Order cancelled
